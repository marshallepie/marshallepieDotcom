Training Philosophy

Martial arts, to me, is not just about self-defense. It's about discovering how to move with intention, clarity, and grace. I teach a system that values precision over power, presence over pressure—rooted in a deep belief that minimal movement can lead to maximum effect.

My Approach

I don’t just teach technique—I guide people through the philosophy of movement. Each session combines physical training with reflective understanding. We learn not only how to act, but when to wait. Not just how to fight, but how to flow.

The Turning Point

One of the most powerful lessons in my martial journey came from a conflict with a teacher. It led me to write a poem that still resonates today:

"My love, regardless..."

This poem is more than a memory. It’s a message—about pride, humility, and choosing peace over ego.

The Art of Laziness

My book-in-progress, The Art of Laziness, is rooted in these ideas. It's a reflection on mastery, technology, and ease—told through stories, like the fight in Brixton, where doing less meant winning more.
